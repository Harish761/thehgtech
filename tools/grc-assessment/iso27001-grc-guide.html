What is the GRC Engine?

TheHGTech Triple Framework GRC Engine is a browser-native assessment platform designed to bridge the gap between technical implementation and regulatory compliance. It allows security teams to map their baseline posture against ISO 27001:2022, NIST CSF 2.0, and CIS Controls v8 within a single session.

Value Proposition: Most GRC tools are expensive, slow, and cloud-heavy. Our engine is FREE, 100% serverless, and provides decision-grade advice in under 15 minutes.

What it DOES:

  • Identifies missing technical and organizational controls.
  • Maps ISO 27001 domains to CIS v8 technical safeguards.
  • Calculates maturity levels for executive-ready decision making.
  • Generates prioritized remediation reports for IT teams.

What it DOES NOT:

Formal Auditor Status: This tool is a Self-Assessment Engine. While it uses current regulatory benchmarks, it is NOT a substitute for a formal external audit by a Certified Lead Auditor.

Evidence Storage: To maintain strict privacy, we do NOT store your sensitive evidence documents. This is a gap assessment tool, not a full GRC evidence repository like Vanta or Drata.

Step-by-Step Assessment

1

Define Your Scope

Launch the Dynamic Scope Builder. Toggle ISO 27001:2022 domains relevant to your environment. Startups typically focus on Operational Controls (Domain 5-8), while established firms should audit all 93 controls.

2

Executive Response Mode

For each control, answering YES assumes you have both a documented policy and proof of implementation. Answering PARTIAL implies the control exists but lacks consistent monitoring or formal documentation.

3

Contextual Rationale

Use the "Consultant Insight" toggle to see WHY a control belongs in your scope. This helps non-GRC managers understand the technical risk behind abstract compliance requirements.

The Scoring Methodology

Our scoring algorithm uses a Weighted Maturity Model to ensure your results represent real-world exposure rather than just "box ticking."

Response Status Weighting Risk Implication
Implemented (Yes) 100% Control is verified and monitored. Low residual risk.
Partial 50% Control exists but is fragile or undocumented. Moderate risk.
Gap (No) 0% Control is missing. High risk exposure detected.

Framework Indicators:

  • ISO 27001:2022: Readiness percentage based on the Annex A 93-control set.
  • NIST CSF 2.0: Alignment based on 106 subcategories across the Core functions.
  • CIS Controls v8: Hygiene score calculated against 153 technical safeguards.

Utilizing Your Reports

Once you reach the Executive Readiness Dashboard, you have two strategic outputs:

A. The Branded PDF Report

Designed for boardrooms and CISOs. This report includes the Maturity Interpretation which translates raw scores into business risk categories (Initial, Developing, or Mature).

B. The Strategic Excel Registry

Designed for technical teams. This file contains the Priority Remediation Checklist. Filter this list by "Risk Level" to assign your top 3 fixes to your DevOps or IT infrastructure team immediately.

ISO Kickstart Tip: Use the Excel export as your Statement of Applicability (SoA) draft. It already contains the control titles, current implementation status, and remediation logic required for the official ISO certification process.
