The Small Business Guide to GRC Success

If you've been asked for "proof of security" by a customer or investor, this guide is your first step. We simplify the complex world of ISO 27001 so you can focus on building your business.

Understanding the Engine

For a small business, "GRC" (Governance, Risk, and Compliance) sounds like a corporate buzzword. In reality, it just means: "Do we have a handle on our security?"

What this tool does for you:

It acts as a Digital Consultant. It asks you the same questions an auditor would, but in a way you can understand. It takes your answers and builds a prioritized list of what to fix first.

What it doesn't do (The Reality Check):

You cannot use this tool to "Self-Certify." ISO 27001 certification still requires an external auditor to physically check your work. Our tool prepares you so that when that auditor arrives, you have already fixed 90% of the problems.

Expectation Setting

As a small team, seeing a low score (e.g., 20%) is normal and expected. Most startups fail early audits because they lack formal "Asset Registers" or "Incident Response Plans." This tool is here to tell you exactly what those are.

How to run an Assessment

1

Build your Scope

Don't try to solve everything at once. Select the domains that feel most critical (usually Operational Controls and Asset Management). You can always come back and add more later.

2

Be Brutally Honest

Answering "Yes" to a question like "Do you have a backup policy?" isn't just about having the backups—it's about having a written document that says how often they happen. If you don't have that document yet, answer "Partial."

3

Read the "Why"

If a question seems confusing, look for the rationale. It explains the specific business risk. For example, a "Logging" control is about being able to see who accessed your data if a hack occurs.

Why are my scores different?

You might notice that after answering the same 20 questions, your ISO Score is 60% but your CIS Score is only 40%. This is intentional. Here is why:

ISO 27001 Settings

Focuses on High-Level Governance. If you have a policy, ISO is generally happy.

NIST CSF 2.0

Focuses on Balance across 6 functions. It checks if you are as good at "Detecting" as you are at "Identifying."

CIS v8 Controls

Focuses on Technical Granularity. CIS is strict—it wants to know exactly how you locked down your servers.

The Multi-Mapping Rule: A single answer about "Passwords" might satisfy one broad ISO requirement, but it only satisfies two out of five specific CIS technical requirements. This is why technical frameworks (CIS) often show lower scores than governance frameworks (ISO).

How to use your outputs

1. The PDF: The "Proof" for Customers

When a prospective customer asks "How is your security?", you send them the Executive PDF. It shows the charts, the maturity level, and the fact that you are aligned with international standards.

2. The Excel: Your Weekend To-Do List

Download the Detailed Excel. Filter the list for "HIGH" risk. Give those 5 tasks to your IT person or DevOps engineer. They contain the "Risk & Consequence" data they need to understand the urgency.

Business Value: Achieving an "Optimized" status in this tool means you are likely in the top 10% of small businesses for security posture. Use this as a competitive advantage.
