The GRC Engine: Documentation

Welcome to the definitive methodology guide for the TheHGTech Triple Framework GRC Engine. This manual covers the scoring logic, multi-framework mapping, and operational utilization of the toolkit.

Critical Expectations

The GRC Engine is a High-Precision Discovery Tool designed for gap analysis and readiness preparation. It is NOT a formal certification platform. An 80% score here does not mean you are ISO 27001 certified; it means your internal controls are statistically prepared for a formal external audit.

Technical Scope & Architecture

Unlike traditional static spreadsheets, the GRC Engine uses a dynamic Control Mapping Matrix. When you answer a single question, the engine calculates shifts across three distinct security philosophies:

ISO 27001:2022

Governance-first. Focuses on the "Management System" and Annex A control presence. It values documented proof and executive accountability.

NIST CSF 2.0

Outcome-based. Maps your controls against the 6 core functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover, and Govern.

CIS Controls v8

Technically granular. 18 Controls and 153 Safeguards. This is the hardest framework to "pass" as it requires specific technical configurations.

Product Workflow

To maximize the accuracy of your assessment, we recommend following the Standard Assessment Cycle:

Phase 1: Dynamic Scoping

The first step is selecting your relevant ISO Domains. The engine will hide irrelevant controls, ensuring a lean, high-velocity audit experience. For most technical firms, Domain 5 (Organizational) and Domain 8 (Technological) are the high-impact starting points.

Phase 2: Maturity Selection

The engine offers three maturity levels per control point:

  • Yes (Full): Control is documented, socialized, and generating logs/proof.
  • Partial: Control is active but lacks formal documentation or regular reviews.
  • No (Gap): Control is non-existent. This triggers "Remediation Insights" in the results.

Phase 3: Consultant Mode

Toggling "Consultant Insights" allows teams to understand the Technical Rationale behind the compliance requirement. This bridges the gap between the CISO's office and the DevOps team.

Scoring Methodology & Variances

A common user question is: "Why is my ISO score 70% but my CIS score only 45%?"

This is the core strength of the GRC Engine. Each framework has a different Audit Tolerance. We use a multi-weighted mapping logic to calculate this variance:

Metric ISO 27001 Logic NIST CSF Logic CIS v8 Logic
Focus Area Policy & Governance Operational Balance Technical Safeguards
Mapping Weight Direct (1:1 per Annex A) Cross-Mapped (1:N) Deep Technical (1:NN)
Scoring Rigor Moderate Strategic Extremely Strict
Maturity Impact Binary (Implemented?) Function-based Safeguard-specific

In essence: CIS v8 requires specific technical proofs. If you answer "Partial" to an ISO control about Access Control, ISO gives you half credit. However, CIS checks for 5 specific technical safeguards under that same topic—if you only do 1 of them, your CIS score will naturally be lower. This provides a realistic view of technical risk versus paper-based compliance.

Deliverables & Output Strategy

The GRC Engine provides two distinct deliverables designed for different stakeholders:

1. The Executive Accountability Report (PDF)

A boardroom-ready document that translates technical gaps into business risk. Use this to secure budget for security tooling or to demonstrate posture to a customer.

2. The Remediation Priority Registry (Excel)

A technical roadmap for engineers. It includes "Strategic Remediation Advice" for every identified gap. This registry should be imported into your task management system (Jira/Asana) as your 90-day security improvement roadmap.
