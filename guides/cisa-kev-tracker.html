What is the CISA KEV Catalog?

The CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog is a living list of Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVEs) that have been actively exploited in the wild. Maintained by the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA), it serves as an authoritative source for prioritizing vulnerability remediation.

Why CISA KEV Matters

Not all CVEs are created equal. While thousands of vulnerabilities are disclosed each year, only a fraction are actively exploited by threat actors. The KEV catalog helps you focus on the vulnerabilities that pose the greatest immediate risk to your organization.

  • Proven Exploitation: Every vulnerability in the KEV catalog has evidence of active exploitation
  • Federal Mandate: Federal agencies are required to remediate KEV vulnerabilities within specific timeframes
  • Industry Standard: Private sector organizations increasingly use KEV as a prioritization framework
  • Regular Updates: CISA continuously adds newly exploited vulnerabilities to the catalog

2. Prioritization Framework

Not all KEV vulnerabilities affect your environment equally. Prioritize based on:

  1. Asset Exposure: Which of your systems are affected?
  2. Exploitability: How easy is it to exploit?
  3. Business Impact: What's the potential damage?
  4. Remediation Deadline: CISA-mandated timelines

3. Remediation Workflow

Establish a clear workflow for addressing KEV vulnerabilities:

  1. Identify affected systems in your environment
  2. Assess risk and prioritize remediation
  3. Apply patches or implement mitigations
  4. Verify remediation effectiveness
  5. Document actions taken

Best Practices

Follow these best practices for effective KEV tracking:

  1. Daily Monitoring: Check for KEV updates at least daily
  2. Automated Alerts: Set up notifications for new additions
  3. Asset Inventory: Maintain accurate inventory of all systems
  4. Patch Management: Have a rapid patch deployment process
  5. Compensating Controls: Implement mitigations when patches aren't immediately available
  6. Documentation: Track all remediation efforts
  7. Regular Reviews: Periodically review your KEV response process

Common Challenges and Solutions

Challenge: Overwhelming Volume

Solution: Focus on vulnerabilities affecting your specific environment. Use automated tools to filter KEV entries based on your asset inventory.

Challenge: Patch Availability

Solution: When patches aren't available, implement compensating controls such as network segmentation, access restrictions, or disabling affected features.

Challenge: Legacy Systems

Solution: For systems that can't be patched, consider isolation, enhanced monitoring, or system replacement planning.
