The 2026 Compliance Checklist: DORA, EU AI Act & NIST Mastery

Comprehensive cheat sheet for navigating 2026's regulatory landscape

22 min read | Intermediate | January 2026
Critical Deadlines:
  • DORA: Full enforcement since January 17, 2025 — audits ongoing
  • EU AI Act (High-Risk): August 2, 2026 — 6 months remaining
  • NIST CSF 2.0: Voluntary but increasingly expected by regulators
Abstract: 2026 marks a critical inflection point in cybersecurity regulation. Financial services organizations must demonstrate DORA compliance under active regulatory scrutiny. AI deployers face EU AI Act obligations for high-risk systems. NIST CSF 2.0's new GOVERN function has become the de facto standard for board-level risk reporting. This guide provides actionable checklists for each framework, identifies overlapping controls to reduce duplication, and offers audit preparation strategies. Target audience: CISOs, GRC managers, compliance officers, and security managers preparing for regulatory assessments.

1. 2026 Regulatory Landscape Overview

The convergence of cybersecurity, operational resilience, and AI governance regulations creates both challenges and opportunities for organizations. Understanding the scope and interactions between frameworks is essential.

Framework Scope Key Deadline Penalty Risk
DORA Digital Operational Resilience Act EU financial entities + ICT providers Active enforcement Up to 2% global turnover
EU AI Act AI systems deployed/developed in EU Aug 2, 2026 (high-risk) Up to €35M or 7% turnover
NIST CSF 2.0 All organizations (voluntary) N/A (best practice) Reputational, contractual

2. DORA Compliance Checklist

The Digital Operational Resilience Act applies to financial entities operating in the EU and their critical ICT service providers.

Pillar 1: ICT Risk Management

ICT risk management framework documented and board-approved
Dedicated ICT security function with clear responsibilities
Business impact analysis covering all critical functions
ICT asset inventory maintained with classification
Encryption standards for data at rest and in transit
Access control with least privilege enforcement
Vulnerability management program with SLAs
Change management procedures documented

Pillar 2: ICT Incident Management

Incident classification scheme aligned with DORA definitions
Major incident reporting to competent authority within 4 hours (initial)
Intermediate report within 72 hours
Final report within 1 month
Root cause analysis process established
Communication templates for regulatory notification

Pillar 3: Digital Operational Resilience Testing

Annual testing program documented
Vulnerability assessments performed quarterly
Penetration testing annually (minimum)
TLPT (Threat-Led Penetration Testing) every 3 years for significant entities
Scenario-based testing including cyber attack simulations
Test results remediation tracking

Pillar 4: ICT Third-Party Risk Management

Register of all ICT third-party providers maintained
Criticality assessment of each provider
Contractual requirements include DORA-mandated clauses
Exit strategies documented for critical providers
Subcontracting chain visibility
Annual due diligence reviews

Pillar 5: Information Sharing

Participation in cyber threat intelligence sharing arrangements
Internal procedures for sharing threat information with authorities

3. EU AI Act Compliance Checklist

The EU AI Act classifies AI systems by risk level. High-risk systems (HR, credit scoring, biometrics, critical infrastructure) face the most stringent requirements.

Deadline Alert: High-risk AI system requirements apply from August 2, 2026. Organizations deploying AI in hiring, lending, law enforcement, or critical infrastructure must comply.

High-Risk AI System Requirements

Risk management system established for AI lifecycle
Training data quality requirements documented
Technical documentation maintained (Annex IV)
Logging/audit trail capabilities enabled
Transparency measures (users informed of AI use)
Human oversight mechanisms in place
Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity measures
Conformity assessment completed
CE marking applied (where required)
Registration in EU database

General-Purpose AI (GPAI) Model Obligations

If you deploy foundation models or LLMs:

Technical documentation including training methodology
Copyright compliance for training data
Summary of training content published
EU AI Office cooperation procedures

Prohibited AI Practices (Effective Feb 2025)

Already Prohibited:
  • Social scoring by public authorities
  • Real-time biometric identification in public spaces (with exceptions)
  • Emotion recognition in workplace/education
  • AI exploiting vulnerabilities (age, disability)
  • Untargeted scraping for facial recognition databases

4. NIST CSF 2.0 Quick Reference

NIST CSF 2.0 introduced the GOVERN function, elevating cybersecurity risk to a board-level concern. The framework now has 6 core functions:

Function Focus Key Activities
GOVERN (New) Strategy & Oversight Risk appetite, roles, policies, supply chain
IDENTIFY Asset Management Inventory, BIA, risk assessment
PROTECT Safeguards Access control, training, data security
DETECT Anomaly Discovery Monitoring, detection processes
RESPOND Incident Handling Response planning, communication
RECOVER Restoration Recovery planning, improvements

GOVERN Function Checklist (New in CSF 2.0)

Organizational context documented (mission, stakeholders, legal)
Risk management strategy with defined risk appetite
Cybersecurity roles and responsibilities defined
Policy framework established and communicated
Board/executive oversight mechanisms
Supply chain risk management program
Continuous improvement processes

5. Control Mapping: One Effort, Three Frameworks

Many controls satisfy multiple frameworks. Efficient organizations implement once and document for each:

Control Domain DORA EU AI Act NIST CSF 2.0
Asset Inventory Art. 8 Annex IV ID.AM
Risk Assessment Art. 6 Art. 9 GV.RM, ID.RA
Incident Response Art. 17-19 Art. 62 RS.RP, RC.RP
Third-Party Risk Art. 28-30 Art. 25 GV.SC
Access Control Art. 9 Art. 14 PR.AA
Logging/Monitoring Art. 10 Art. 12 DE.CM
Testing Art. 24-27 Art. 9 ID.RA
Efficiency Tip: Build your control library once using NIST CSF 2.0 as the baseline, then map to DORA and EU AI Act requirements. This reduces duplicate documentation by 40-60%.

6. Audit Preparation Strategies

30-Day Audit Readiness Plan

Week Focus Area Deliverables
Week 1 Documentation Review Policy inventory, gap analysis, evidence index
Week 2 Control Testing Internal control tests, remediation of gaps
Week 3 Stakeholder Prep Interview scripts, RACI for audit queries
Week 4 Mock Audit Dry-run walkthrough, final remediation

Common Audit Findings to Avoid

  • Stale documentation: Policies not updated within 12 months
  • Evidence gaps: Controls documented but no proof of execution
  • Incomplete vendor assessments: Critical providers without due diligence
  • Missing board reporting: No evidence of executive oversight
  • Testing gaps: No penetration test in >12 months
