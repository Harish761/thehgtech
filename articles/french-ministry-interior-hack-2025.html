French Ministry of Interior Hacked: 22-Year-Old Arrested

Email systems compromised December 11-12. Hacker "Indra" claims access to police, tax, and criminal databases. Suspect arrested within days.

Event: Dec 11-12, 2025 Published: Dec 18, 2025 5 min read
Key Alert: The French Ministry of Interior has confirmed a cyberattack on its internal email servers. A 22-year-old suspect has been arrested, but the full scope of the breach is still under investigation.

What Happened

The French Ministry of Interior confirmed on December 17, 2025 that threat actors successfully breached its internal email systems between December 11-12, 2025. The attack allowed unauthorized access to email communications and some document files.

A hacker using the alias "Indra" has claimed responsibility for the attack, alleging far broader access than the Ministry has officially acknowledgedâ€”including purported access to police systems, tax databases, and criminal records.

Arrest Made

French authorities moved quickly, arresting a 22-year-old individual in connection with the attack. The arrest came just days after the breach was detected, suggesting either strong forensic capabilities or prior intelligence on the suspect.

Hacker Claims: The individual claiming to be "Indra" has alleged access to:
  • Police investigation databases
  • Tax authority records
  • Criminal record systems
  • Internal government communications

Note: The Ministry has not confirmed these broader claims.

Why This Matters

This breach represents a significant attack on French government infrastructure. If the hacker's claims of accessing police and criminal databases are verified, this could rank among the most serious government breaches in European history.

Key concerns include:

  • Law Enforcement Compromise: Potential exposure of ongoing investigations
  • Personal Data: Millions of French citizens' records potentially at risk
  • National Security: Intelligence and security communications may have been accessed
  • Political Sensitivity: Attack timing raises questions about motives

What to Watch

The investigation is ongoing. Key questions remain:

  • Did the arrested individual act alone or as part of a group?
  • What was the initial attack vector (phishing, vulnerability exploitation)?
  • How much data was actually exfiltrated?
  • Will stolen data appear on dark web markets?
