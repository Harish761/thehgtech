Covenant Health Ransomware: Qilin Steals 850GB from 478,000 Patients

A Massachusetts healthcare provider. 478,000 patients. 1.3 million files. 850 gigabytes. The Qilin ransomware gang's latest victim shows why healthcare remains cybercrime's most vulnerable target.

Breach: May 2025 Published: Jan 2, 2026 20 min read
BREACH CONFIRMED: Covenant Health, Inc. (Andover, Massachusetts) has confirmed a data breach originally discovered in May 2025 now affects over 478,000 individuals. The Qilin ransomware group claims to have exfiltrated more than 1.3 million files totaling 850GB.

The Angle: Healthcare Under Siege

Every week brings another healthcare data breach. But the Covenant Health attack deserves attention because it exemplifies everything wrong with healthcare cybersecurity in 2026:

  • Massive data volume: 850GB is not a smash-and-grab—it's systematic exfiltration
  • Sensitive data types: Names, SSNs, medical records, insurance information
  • Prolific threat actor: Qilin is one of the most active ransomware groups operating today
  • Discovery timeline: Breach in May, full impact disclosed in January

The uncomfortable truth: Healthcare organizations are being breached faster than they can improve their defenses. And patients are paying the price.

Who is Qilin?

Qilin Ransomware Group
Also Known As: Agenda ransomware
First Observed: August 2022
Model: Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS)
Primary Targets: Healthcare, education, manufacturing
Tactics: Double extortion (encryption + data theft)
Languages: Rust, Go (cross-platform)
2025 Activity: Among top 5 most active ransomware groups

Qilin has been particularly aggressive in targeting healthcare organizations. Their attacks on NHS suppliers, healthcare networks, and medical providers have earned them a reputation as one of the most dangerous threats to the sector.

Why Healthcare?

Ransomware groups like Qilin target healthcare for predictable reasons:

  1. Life-critical operations: Hospitals can't afford downtime—pressure to pay is immense
  2. Sensitive data: Medical records fetch premium prices on dark web markets
  3. Legacy systems: Outdated infrastructure is easier to breach
  4. Underfunded security: Healthcare IT budgets lag other industries
  5. Regulatory pressure: HIPAA violations add further incentive to pay quietly

What Was Stolen

According to Covenant Health's disclosure and Qilin's claims, the compromised data includes:

Why This Data is Dangerous:
  • Identity theft: SSNs enable full identity fraud
  • Medical identity theft: Stolen insurance info used for fraudulent claims
  • Blackmail: Sensitive diagnoses (mental health, HIV, etc.) used for extortion
  • Targeted phishing: Detailed personal info enables convincing scams
  • Permanent exposure: Unlike credit cards, SSNs and medical history can't be replaced

Attack Analysis

Initial Access

While Covenant Health hasn't disclosed specifics, Qilin's typical attack vectors include:

  • Phishing emails: Credential harvesting or malware delivery
  • Exposed RDP: Internet-facing Remote Desktop Protocol with weak credentials
  • VPN vulnerabilities: Exploiting unpatched VPN appliances
  • Compromised credentials: Purchased from initial access brokers

The Exfiltration: 850GB Over Time

Exfiltrating 850GB of data doesn't happen instantly. This represents prolonged access to Covenant Health's network:

  • At 100 Mbps upload speed, 850GB takes approximately 19 hours to transfer
  • At more realistic speeds to avoid detection, this could take days or weeks
  • 1.3 million files suggests access to multiple systems and file shares
  • This volume indicates comprehensive network compromise, not limited access
Key Questions:
  • Why wasn't this data exfiltration detected?
  • Was there data loss prevention (DLP) in place?
  • How long did attackers have access before discovery?
  • Were backup systems also compromised?

Lessons for Healthcare Organizations

Immediate Actions

Security Priorities:
  1. Deploy EDR/XDR: Modern endpoint detection is essential
  2. Implement DLP: Detect large data transfers before completion
  3. Network segmentation: Limit lateral movement and data access
  4. Patch management: Timely patching of internet-facing systems
  5. MFA everywhere: Especially on VPN, email, and admin accounts

Detection Opportunities

An 850GB exfiltration should trigger multiple alerts:

  • Network anomaly detection: Unusual outbound data volumes
  • File access monitoring: Mass file access from single account
  • Egress monitoring: Large uploads to unknown destinations
  • After-hours activity: Data transfers outside business hours
  • Cloud storage uploads: Sudden use of unauthorized cloud services

For Affected Patients

If you're among the 478,000+ affected individuals:

Immediate Steps

  1. Freeze your credit: Contact Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion to freeze credit reports
  2. Monitor for fraud: Review credit reports, bank statements, and medical bills
  3. Use offered services: Accept any credit monitoring Covenant Health provides
  4. Be alert for phishing: Expect targeted scams using your stolen information
  5. Review medical statements: Check for services you didn't receive (medical identity theft)

Long-Term Vigilance

  • Your SSN is permanently exposed—maintain credit freezes indefinitely
  • Medical identity theft can surface years later—keep monitoring
  • Document everything for potential legal action or claims
