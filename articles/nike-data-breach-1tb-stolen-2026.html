Nike Data Breach: 1.4TB of Internal Designs & Employee Data Stolen

Threat actors claim "structural access" to Nike's internal network via a supply chain compromise, exposing unreleased product designs and thousands of employee records.

TheHGTech Security Team January 31, 2026 5 min read Data Breach
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Victim: Nike Inc.
Data Exfiltrated: 1.4 Terabytes
Type of Data: Internal R&D, CAD Designs, Employee PII, Contracts
Threat Status: Data partially leaked. Investigation ongoing.
Attack Vector: Third-party vendor compromise (Supply Chain).

Nike is currently investigating a massive cybersecurity incident after threat actors posted 1.4TB of internal data on a dark web forum. The breach, which allegedly occurred in mid-January 2026, appears to have exposed highly sensitive intellectual property, including designs for unreleased sneakers and apparel planned for 2027.

The attackers, a known ransomware affiliate group, claimed to have maintained "structural access" to Nike's internal network for weeks, allowing them to map the infrastructure and exfiltrate data without detection.

What Was Stolen?

Based on the sample file tree released by the attackers as proof-of-concept, the stolen data includes:

  • R&D Assets: 3D CAD files and technical specs for upcoming "Smart Footwear" lines.
  • Employee PII: Payroll records, home addresses, and SSNs for thousands of US-based employees.
  • Legal Documents: Non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) with high-profile athletes and celebrity partners.
  • Network Maps: Detailed diagrams of internal server topology, which could aid future attacks.

The Attack Vector: Supply Chain Weakness

How They Got In

Preliminary analysis suggests the attackers did not breach Nike's perimeter directly. Instead, they compromised a secure file transfer server (SFTP) used by a third-party logistics partner.

From there, they harvested credentials that allowed them to pivot into Nike's "Partner Portal," eventually locating a misconfigured internal API gateway that trusted traffic from the partner network.

Industry Impact

This breach highlights the critical risk of "implicit trust" in supply chain relationships. Even with world-class perimeter defense, a compromised vendor with legitimate access privileges can serve as a trojan horse.
