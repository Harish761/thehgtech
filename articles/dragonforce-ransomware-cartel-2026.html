DragonForce: The New Ransomware Cartel Built on LockBit and Conti DNA

From pro-Palestinian hacktivists to profit-driven ransomware cartel. DragonForce has emerged as a formidable threat by combining leaked code from LockBit 3.0 and Conti into a powerful RaaS platform that's already claimed high-profile victims.

Threat Active: 2023 - Present Published: Jan 20, 2026 18 min read By Harish G
DragonForce Ransomware Cartel | LockBit 3.0 + Conti DNA | RaaS Platform
ACTIVE THREAT: DragonForce has evolved from a hacktivist collective into one of the most aggressive ransomware cartels of 2026. By leveraging leaked source code from LockBit 3.0 and Conti, they've built a sophisticated RaaS operation that's absorbing rival groups and targeting high-value organizations worldwide.

The Evolution: From Hacktivists to Criminal Enterprise

DragonForce didn't start as a ransomware operation. The group emerged in 2023 as a pro-Palestinian hacktivist collective, conducting DDoS attacks and website defacements against Israeli targets. But somewhere along the way, the operators realized there was more money in extortion than ideology.

By late 2023, DragonForce had pivoted to ransomware, and by March 2025, they formally rebranded as a "cartel" — launching an affiliate program that invites other cybercriminals to use their platform in exchange for a cut of the profits.

The DNA: LockBit 3.0 + Conti Source Code

What makes DragonForce particularly dangerous is their ransomware's genetic makeup. Rather than building from scratch, the group has incorporated leaked source code from two of history's most successful ransomware operations:

LockBit 3.0 (LockBit Black)

When a disgruntled LockBit developer leaked the LockBit 3.0 source code in September 2022, it became a gift that keeps giving to the cybercriminal ecosystem. DragonForce has heavily leveraged this code, which included:

  • Advanced encryption routines: Fast, efficient file encryption that minimizes detection time
  • Anti-analysis features: Techniques to evade security researchers and sandboxes
  • Self-propagation capabilities: Ability to spread laterally through networks
  • Credential harvesting: Built-in tools to extract passwords and tokens

Conti Source Code

After the Conti group imploded following their attack on Costa Rica in 2022, their source code also found its way into the wild. DragonForce has incorporated Conti's:

  • Network reconnaissance modules: Sophisticated scanning for valuable targets
  • Active Directory exploitation: Tools for domain compromise
  • Data exfiltration pipelines: Efficient extraction of sensitive files
  • Negotiation playbooks: Proven tactics for extracting payments
Why This Matters: By combining code from two highly successful operations, DragonForce has essentially created a "greatest hits" ransomware that incorporates years of criminal R&D without the original development costs.

The Cartel Model: RaaS 2.0

DragonForce's cartel model represents an evolution in Ransomware-as-a-Service. Their platform, called "Ransombay," offers affiliates a comprehensive toolkit:

Feature Description
Ransomware Builder Customizable payloads with LockBit/Conti DNA
Leak Site Hosting Centralized infrastructure for victim shaming
Negotiation Portal Professional chat interface for ransom discussions
Data Analysis Service Team reviews stolen data to identify leverage points
Payment Processing Cryptocurrency laundering and distribution
Technical Support 24/7 assistance for affiliates

Revenue Sharing

Like other RaaS operations, DragonForce takes a percentage of each ransom payment. Reports suggest they operate on a 70/30 or 80/20 split favoring affiliates, which makes them competitive in the crowded ransomware market.

Absorbing the Competition

What truly distinguishes DragonForce's cartel approach is their strategy of absorbing rival operations. Rather than competing with smaller ransomware groups, they're offering to bring them under the DragonForce umbrella — providing better infrastructure, more victims, and shared resources. This consolidation strategy mirrors legitimate business practices and suggests sophisticated leadership.

Notable Victims: UK Retail Under Siege

DragonForce made international headlines in 2025 when they launched a coordinated campaign against major UK retailers:

Marks & Spencer

The iconic British retailer was hit in early 2025, with DragonForce claiming to have exfiltrated customer data and internal documents. The attack disrupted operations and forced temporary system shutdowns.

The Co-operative Group (Co-op)

The UK's fifth-largest food retailer suffered a significant breach. DragonForce threatened to release employee data, supplier contracts, and financial information.

Harrods

The luxury department store was targeted in the same campaign, demonstrating DragonForce's confidence in taking on high-profile, well-resourced targets.

Attack Methodology: Multi-Extortion Tactics

DragonForce employs a four-pronged extortion strategy that maximizes pressure on victims:

1. Encrypt Files 2. Steal Data 3. Threaten Leak 4. Contact Victims

1. File Encryption

Traditional ransomware encryption renders files unusable until a decryption key is purchased. DragonForce's encryption is fast and thorough, prioritizing high-value file types.

2. Data Exfiltration

Before encryption, DragonForce systematically identifies and extracts sensitive data — financial records, customer databases, employee information, intellectual property, and trade secrets.

3. Public Leaks

Stolen data is threatened for release on their leak site. They often release "samples" to prove they have the data and demonstrate the consequences of non-payment.

4. Direct Communication

DragonForce doesn't just threaten — they act. Affiliates have been known to directly contact employees, partners, and even customers to amplify pressure on victim organizations.

Data Analysis Service: In August 2025, DragonForce introduced a "data analysis service" where their team reviews stolen data to identify specific leverage points — executive communications, legal issues, regulatory violations — that can be used to increase ransom demands. This professionalized approach to extortion represents a concerning escalation.

The Broader Ransomware Landscape in 2026

DragonForce's rise occurs against a evolving ransomware backdrop. Key trends include:

LockBit's Resurrection

Despite Operation Cronos disrupting LockBit in early 2024, the group announced a comeback in September 2025 with "LockBit 5.0" and is actively recruiting new affiliates. They remain a major threat, particularly to healthcare organizations.

Code Sharing Economy

The leaking of LockBit and Conti source code has democratized ransomware development. Groups like DragonForce can build sophisticated operations without starting from zero.

Geographic Expansion

For the first time in 2026, analysts predict that new ransomware actors operating outside Russia will outnumber those within it. This global expansion makes attribution and law enforcement more challenging.

AI Integration

Ransomware groups are beginning to integrate AI capabilities into their operations — from automated reconnaissance to AI-powered social engineering. This trend is expected to accelerate throughout 2026.

Defense Recommendations

Protecting against DragonForce and similar cartels requires a multi-layered approach:

Immediate Actions:
  • Patch aggressively: DragonForce exploits known vulnerabilities. Prioritize CISA KEV catalog items.
  • Implement MFA everywhere: Credential theft is a primary access vector.
  • Segment networks: Limit lateral movement once access is gained.
  • Monitor for data exfiltration: Large outbound transfers are a red flag.
  • Maintain offline backups: 3-2-1 backup strategy with tested restoration.
  • Train employees: Phishing remains a common initial access vector.

Detection Indicators

Organizations should monitor for:

  • Unusual process execution patterns consistent with LockBit/Conti TTPs
  • Attempts to disable security tools or backup solutions
  • Large-scale file enumeration across network shares
  • Connections to known DragonForce infrastructure (check threat feeds)
  • Use of legitimate tools (PsExec, Cobalt Strike) for lateral movement

Incident Response Preparation

Given DragonForce's multi-extortion model, organizations should prepare for scenarios where:

  • Data has been stolen regardless of whether encryption occurred
  • Attackers may contact employees, partners, or customers directly
  • Regulatory notification may be required even without ransom payment
  • Reputation management becomes as important as technical recovery

Key Takeaways

Summary:
  1. DragonForce has evolved from hacktivists to one of the most aggressive ransomware cartels of 2026
  2. Their ransomware combines leaked LockBit 3.0 and Conti source code for maximum effectiveness
  3. The cartel model includes a RaaS platform, affiliate program, and absorption of rival groups
  4. Multi-extortion tactics include encryption, data theft, leak threats, and direct victim contact
  5. High-profile UK retailers Marks & Spencer, Co-op, and Harrods have been targeted
  6. Defense requires aggressive patching, network segmentation, and preparation for data theft scenarios
